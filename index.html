Upholding Dignity: The 10 Precepts

Wearing the uniform means representing TCCA values. Uniformed members commit to following ten basic guidelines of mindful conduct.

Includes: No killing (cherish all life and promote peace), no stealing, and upholding societal laws. Fosters safe, caring, and ethical environments.

Includes: No lying (promoting trust, honesty, and speaking wholesome words) and practicing relationship mindfulness (being respectful and upright in personal relationships).

Includes: No drinking alcohol, no smoking (including vaping/tobacco), and no gambling. Fosters a clean, supportive, substance-free collegiate family.

Includes: Adhering to traffic regulations, practicing filial piety (respecting and supporting parents & elders), and committing to a compassionate vegetarian/vegan diet to protect our planet.

Graduation Sash & Active Status Requirements

To celebrate your collegiate service, TCCANE awards official Tzu Ching Graduation Sashes. To earn your sash, you must remain active in your final year by attending at least five events/meetings (or 50% of your chapter's hosted events) and participating in at least one regional event or retreat.

Officer & E-Board Eligibility Rules

TCCANE officers are elected annually in March or April. E-board members are highly encouraged to be uniformed Tzu Ching. Candidates for President and Vice President must have been active members for at least one semester and participated in at least two service events. Campus advisors mentor student officers but do not appoint them.

The Academic Year

Annual Milestones & Key Events

Here is a glance at the standard annual timeline. TCCANE offers opportunities for growth all year round.

Aug - Sept

National TCCA Conference

Kickstart the new academic year! Chapters complete registrations, and student leads attend the National training camp to exchange service ideas.

Oct - Nov

Fall Orientations

Local chapters host welcome sessions and orientations. We welcome prospective volunteers, introduce our missions, and launch local service activities.

December

Global Conference in Taiwan

Uniformed Tzu Chings can apply to represent the Northeast Region at the international TCCA summit in Hualien, Taiwan, learning from global volunteers.

May

Northeast TCCA Graduation

We honor graduating seniors with Tzu Chi sashes, celebrate our annual impact, elect incoming officers, and reflect on our shared growth.

Tzu Chi Terminology

We honor our advisors, mentors, and older peers with respectful terms that emphasize a unified family culture. Hover to learn:

Shī gū (SG) "Dharma Aunt" — Female adult volunteer advisor
Shī bó (SB) "Dharma Uncle" — Male adult volunteer advisor
Xué jiě (XJ) "Senior Sister" — Alumni female mentor
Xué zhǎng (XZ) "Senior Brother" — Alumni male mentor
