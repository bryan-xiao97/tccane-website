For a prospective college student interested in joining or leading a chapter within the Northeast Region of the Tzu Chi Collegiate Association (TCCA), the following executive summary outlines the essential policies, expectations, and organizational structure to help you navigate your journey as a Tzu Ching.

### **At a Glance: Becoming a Tzu Ching**

Joining TCCA offers a path from being a "new member" (general volunteer) to becoming a "Tzu Ching"—a uniformed member of the collegiate association. This transition is marked by a commitment to the organization's core missions: Charity, Medicine, Education, and Humanistic Culture.

---

### **Core Policies & Requirements**

To fully engage with the association and represent its values, students must adhere to specific eligibility and behavioral standards.

* **Uniform Eligibility:** To earn the official blue-and-white uniform, you must:  
  * Complete **20 service hours** across at least three different event types.  
  * Attend at least one **retreat, orientation, or TCCA conference**.  
  * Receive approval from a chapter advisor and submit a verified hours log.  
* **The 10 Precepts:** Wearing the uniform is a commitment to upholding Tzu Chi’s dignity. You must follow the 10 Precepts, which include no killing, stealing, lying, drinking, smoking, gambling, or participating in political demonstrations, as well as practicing filial piety and consuming a vegetarian diet.  
* **Active Status & Graduation Sashes:** To receive a sash at graduation, you must be a uniformed member who remains active by attending at least **five events/meetings** (or 50% of hosted events) during your final school year. This must include at least one regional event or retreat.

### **Engagement & Leadership Opportunities**

The Northeast Region (NE) supports campus chapters at **NYU** and **Stony Brook**, as well as community chapters in **Boston** and **NYC** for students whose schools do not have a dedicated TCCA club.

* **Executive Board (E-Board) Roles:** Chapters are student-led, with roles including President, Vice President, Secretary, Treasurer, Event Coordinator, and Social Media Chair.  
  * **Requirements:** Candidates for E-Board should be uniformed. Presidents and Vice Presidents must have been members for at least one semester and participated in at least two events.  
  * **Elections:** These are held annually in March or April. Advisors may mentor but **cannot** select the E-Board; they must be elected by members.  
* **Annual Milestones:**  
  * **Aug/Sept:** National TCCA Conference and chapter registration.  
  * **Oct/Nov:** Fall Orientation for new members.  
  * **Dec:** Potential Global TCCA Conference in Taiwan.  
  * **May:** NE TCCA Graduation Ceremony.

### **Essential Cultural Facts**

* **Terminology:** You will often hear honorifics like **Shī gū (SG)** and **Shī bó (SB)** for adult volunteers, and **Xué jiě (XJ)** or **Xué zhǎng (XZ)** for alumni mentors.  
* **Eco-Consciousness:** Sustainability is a key value. Members are encouraged to bring an **Environmental Protection Kit (EPK)**—reusable bowl, cup, and utensils—to all events, as single-use dishware is not used.  
* **Advisor Support:** Every chapter has advisors who act as mentors to empower student leaders rather than direct them.

### **Service & Support Resources**

* **Regional Contacts:** For any questions regarding involvement or starting a chapter, you can reach out to the Northeast Regional Advisors at **tcca.neregion@us.tzuchi.org**.  
* **Professional Development:** TCCA provides verification for service hours and letters of recommendation for graduate school or job applications, provided you give 2–4 weeks' notice and submit the required documentation.
